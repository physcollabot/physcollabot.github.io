Call for Contributions

Extended abstracts on tightly coupled collaboration

We plan to solicit high-quality 2-4 page extended abstracts on the theory, algorithms, and deployment of multi-robot systems that collaborate through physical interaction.

Scope

Topics of interest

Submissions should address tight physical coupling and interaction in multi-robot systems operating toward complex tasks in real-world environments.

Collaborative manipulation
Multi-robot task allocation and scheduling
Task and motion planning
Learning-based coordination
Sim-to-real transfer for multi-robot systems
Navigation among movable obstacles
Leveraging single-agent policy for team collaboration
Collaborative perception in multi-robot systems

Submission format

  • 2-4 page extended abstracts, including references.
  • Formatted following the IROS paper guidelines.
  • Intended for poster presentation and discussion at the workshop.

Review process

  • Single-blind review by a committee with expertise in planning, control, and learning.
  • Assessment based on quality, relevance, novelty, and clarity.
  • Program quality supported by invited speakers and a cross-disciplinary organizer team.

Best paper / poster recognition

  • A best paper or best poster award is planned to encourage strong submissions.
  • Final selection will involve invited speakers and senior organizers.
  • Accepted contributions will remain visible beyond the event on the workshop website.

Who should submit

Work that moves beyond loosely coupled coordination

We are especially interested in work that deals with contact-rich coordination, shared physical constraints, long-horizon dependencies, coupled manipulation, collaborative transport, or methods that connect learning and structure for physically interactive teams.

Strong submissions can be theoretical, algorithmic, system-oriented, or experimental, but should clearly articulate why the problem requires genuinely coupled multi-robot reasoning.

Dissemination

Accepted materials will stay accessible

The workshop website is planned as an open repository for accepted abstracts, slides, and other workshop materials. This ensures that poster contributions remain easy to revisit and cite after the conference.
