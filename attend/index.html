A workshop for researchers building real collaborative autonomy

PhysCollaBot targets the researchers and practitioners working at the interface of multi-robot systems, planning, control, learning, and embodied intelligence, with an emphasis on settings where physical interaction matters.

IROS 2026

Intended Audience

Who should attend

Multi-robot systems researchers

Attendees working on coordination, collaborative manipulation, task allocation, or collective execution will find a focused forum around physical coupling.

Planning and control communities

The workshop explicitly targets motion planning, task and motion planning, optimization, and whole-body control perspectives.

Robot learning and embodied AI

We also expect strong participation from researchers exploring data-efficient learning, adaptation, and transferring single-robot intelligence to teams.

Target Communities

Relevant technical committees

  • IEEE RAS TC on Model-based Optimization for RoboticsRelevant to structured planning and optimization under physical constraints.
  • IEEE RAS TC on Multi-Robot SystemsCentral to the workshop’s focus on collaborative autonomy.
  • IEEE RAS TC on Robot LearningRelevant to data-driven approaches for coordination and collaboration.
  • IEEE RAS TC on Whole-Body ControlRelevant to coordinated manipulation, force control, and embodied physical interaction.

Expected Scale

Attendance estimate

Based on related workshops in multi-robot systems, planning, and collaborative robotics, we expect roughly 100-150 attendees and 10-20 accepted poster abstracts. The event is intended to be discussion-heavy rather than lecture-only.

This estimate is informed by recent related workshops at IROS, ICRA, and RSS on multi-agent planning, multi-robot learning, construction, and logistics-oriented multi-robot autonomy.
