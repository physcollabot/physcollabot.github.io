Attend

A workshop for real collaborative autonomy

PhysCollaBot targets the researchers and practitioners working at the interface of multi-robot systems, planning, control, learning, and embodied intelligence, with an emphasis on settings where physical interaction matters.

IROS 2026 in Pittsburgh Invited talks, posters, and panel discussion
Conference Context

Workshop dates at IROS 2026

PhysCollaBot will take place on one of the IROS 2026 workshop days, September 27, 2026 or October 1, 2026. Final room and day assignment will appear here once published by the conference.

IROS 2026

Intended Audience

Who should attend

Multi-robot systems researchers

Attendees working on coordination, collaborative manipulation, task allocation, or collective execution will find a focused forum around physical coupling.

Planning and control communities

The workshop explicitly targets motion planning, task and motion planning, optimization, and whole-body control perspectives.

Robot learning and embodied AI

We also expect strong participation from researchers exploring data-efficient learning, adaptation, and transferring single-robot intelligence to teams.

Participation Format

What attendees can expect

Invited talks with discussion

Each invited talk is positioned to spark technical exchange across planning, control, learning, and embodied intelligence.

Poster sessions and networking

Accepted abstracts will be presented in interactive poster sessions designed for deep technical conversation.

Panels across communities

Panel discussions will focus on shared abstractions, benchmarks, and deployment challenges for physical collaboration.

Dissemination

Materials will remain online after the event

Accepted abstracts, slides, and any shared workshop materials will be archived on this website for open access by the broader robotics community.
